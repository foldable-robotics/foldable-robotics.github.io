Design and Manufacturing Workflow

Team Assignment

Introduction

The purpose of this assignment is to plan for the manufacturing of the full system, test the output, and use it to produce a prototype.

Instructions

  1. Create a quick paper mockup of your team’s prototype design. Once you have arrived at your desired architecture, proceed to unfold the prototype in a way that makes sense from the perspective of optimizing material usage, staying within any boundary limitations (size of a piece of posterboard, size of the cutter you will be using).

  2. Design the geometry of your robot in .dxf format. Suggested workflow:

    a. Convert the flattened pattern to a dimensioned Solidworks sketch. Include any mounting holes for motors, springs, or connectors. a. Use the Solidworks tutorial to create a hinged assembly of all parts of the design a. Take a screenshot of the robot in its folded & assembled state a. Flatten the assembly back to its original flattened state a. Create a drawing from the assembly and use the solidworks export macro to export a yaml file (generic). a. Use the solidworks support functionality in the foldable_robotics package to convert to a dxf a. Modify the .dxf as needed to specify any special cut lines, holes or other layers not automatically identified.

  1. Using a single-layer design approach, compute the design of your device in one layer, plotting each step along the way. This should include:

    1. a one-layer hinge design that fits your team’s need (with justification for material used, rotational needs, manufacturing method used, etc)
    2. mapping the hinge design to each joint in your joints layer of the dxf
    3. subtracting the one layer hinge design from your body layer
    4. holes computed for any vertices

  2. Using a 5-layer design approach, compute the same design of your device in five layers, plotting each step along the way. This should include:

    1. a five-layer hinge design that fits your team’s need (with justification for material used, rotational needs, manufacturing method used, etc)
    2. mapping the hinge design to each joint in your joints layer of the dxf
    3. subtracting the 5-layer hinge design from the body laminate
    4. holes computed for any vertices

  3. Using the full design pipeline found on the website and discussed in class, compute the manufacturing geometry for a five-layer laminate, plotting each step along the way. This should include:

    1. Web design
    2. Support design
    3. Non-removable scrap
    4. Connection check of all parts that result from the second-pass cut.
    5. Similarity check between design and removed final part.

  4. Export your final cut files to .dxf or .pdf, depending on your need. You should export one file per layer as well as one final cut file(if using a laminate process).

Submission

Please include:

  1. The picture of your paper mockup in its a. folded and a. flattened state
  2. A Solidworks screenshot of your robot in its a. folded and a. flattened state
  3. The dxf of your body and joint layers output by solidworks or designed by you.
  4. Your jupyter notebook script consisting of the following elements, clearly labeled. a. 1-Layer Robot Design a. 5-Layer Robot Design a. 5-Layer Manufacturing Design
  5. Final .dxf files

Please follow the “Submission Best Practices” document posted on Canvas. This assignment should be submitted to canvas as a .pdf document as well as a jupyter notebook (.ipynb). If submitted as a notebook, make sure it is fully compiled. This can be done by opening the file in the jupyter browser and in the top jupyter menu selecting “Kernel” –> “Restart and Run All”.

Supporting files should accompany the jupyter notebook in a .zip file of the entire folder.
