    Parts and Materials

    The goal of my research is to make it possible to make a ~$100 robot in less than two hours.

    • We will mix in prototyping throughout the semester
    • We will use a mixture of digital and analog prototyping approaches.

    Comparison

    Aspect Rigid Flexible Soft
    Joints mechanical joints soft links or joints with springs No joints; weaker or stiffer regions
    Strain none bending stretching
    Materials stiff mixed soft
    DOFs fully actuated specific, passive DOFs completely soft
    Dynamics simple more complex intractable for common use

    Foldable Mechanisms

    one type of flexible mechanism

    Foldable Mechanisms

    • Use affordable materials
    • Accessible, planar fabrication techniques
    • Compliance accompanies material / geometry choice
    • Can add benefits of flexible systems to the precision rigid, kinematics-driven systems

    SCRAM Mechanisms

    In this class

    we will study

    • traditional mechanisms
      • how to prototype & analyze them
    • flexible material systems
      • how to build flexibility into selected materials
    • rapid prototyping techniques
      • how to compute and fabricate flexible robots
    • dynamics of flexible systems
      • how to create simple but sufficient models
    • experimental characterization, validation, and optimization
      • how to close the design loop
